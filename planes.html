Product spotlight

Top Flite F4U corsair

This RC plane is a scaled model of the Vought F4U Corsair, an American fighter aircraft used primarily during WWII. It was used between 1942 and 1953. It had a maximum speed of 446 MPH, a range of 1,000 miles, and a maximum climb of 41,500 feet. The scaled model has a fuselage length of 5.5 feet with a 7-foot wingspan.

Specifications

  • Model Number: TOPA0410
  • Wingspan: 86.5 inches
  • Fuselage length: 67 inches
  • Weight: 24-26 pounds
  • Requires: 2.1-2.8 cu in glow engine or 2.5-4.25 cu in gas engine, 6 channel radio w/10 servos (7 heavy-duty servos recommended), flaps, retractable landing gear and tailwheel

    • Japanese Zero Fighter

    The Japanese Zero Fighter is a scaled RC model of the zero fighter aircraft used by the Japanese in WWII between 1940 and 1945. It had a maximum speed of 346 MPH, a range of 2,000 miles, and a maximum climb of 33,000 feet. This scaled model has a fuselage length of 4 feet and almost a 6-foot wingspan.

    • The Spirit of St. Louis

    This RC plane is a scaled model of the infamous plane flown by Charles Lindbergh for the first solo non-stop transatlantic flight. Lindbergh flew the Spirit of St. Louis from Long Island, NY to Paris, France in May of 1927. The Spirit of St. Louis is a ready-to-fly model with a fuselage length of a foot and a wingspan of 2 feet.
